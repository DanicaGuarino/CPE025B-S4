Hot Matcha Latte: A Creamy Japanese Classic

This steaming hot beverage combines premium green tea powder with velvety milk. Known for its rich antioxidants and smooth, sweet flavor, a homemade matcha latte offers a meditative preparation process that rivals any café experience.

A warm hug in a mug. Experience the perfect balance of earthy stone-ground tea and velvet micro-foam for a morning that starts with clarity.

- Danica Guariño, The Matcha Expert

Ingredients:

  • Matcha Powder: 1½ teaspoons
  • Hot Water: 2 tablespoons (80°C)
  • Milk: ¾ cup (Dairy or Plant-based)
  • Sugar: ½ teaspoon (Optional)

Preparation:

  1. Sift the matcha through a fine-mesh strainer into a wide cup or bowl.
  2. Add the hot water and optional sugar to the powder.
  3. Whisk briskly in a "W" motion using a chasen until no lumps remain.
  4. Heat and froth the milk until foamy (about 65°C).
  5. Pour the steamed milk into the center of the matcha slurry and serve.

Cooking Tips:

  • 1 teaspoon of matcha (instead of 1.5) creates a more subtle, lighter taste.
  • 10-15 seconds of whisking with a handheld frother creates the perfect airy mouthfeel.
  • 80 degrees Celsius is the ideal water temperature to ensure no bitterness.
  • Oat milk is the best plant-based alternative for achieving a stable, thick foam.
Recipe Details
Ingredient Quantity
Matcha Powder 1½ tsp
Hot Water 2 tbsp
Milk ¾ cup
Sweetener ½ tsp

Preparation Time: 5 minutes

Serving Suggestions: Sprinkle extra matcha powder on top for a professional finish.

Iced Matcha Latte: A Refreshing Summer Ritual

Iced Matcha Latte takes just minutes to make but will keep you energized all day long. This creamy and refreshing green tea powder drink is packed with antioxidants and delivers a concentrated source of amino acids.

Beat the heat with a vibrant splash of emerald. Clean energy meets cool refreshment in this caffeinated classic made for sunny afternoons.

- Danica Guariño, The Matcha Expert

Ingredients:

  • Matcha Powder: 1½ teaspoons
  • Hot Water: 2 tablespoons (80°C)
  • Milk: ¾ cup (Whole, 2%, or Plant-based)
  • Simple Syrup: 1-2 teaspoons (To taste)

Preparation:

  1. Add ice cubes, milk, and simple syrup to a glass cup.
  2. Whisk the matcha and hot water in a separate bowl until no lumps remain.
  3. Continue whisking until the mixture is smooth and a little foamy.
  4. Pour the matcha mixture into the milk glass.
  5. Stir gently to combine and serve immediately.

Cooking Tips:

  • 176 degrees Fahrenheit (80°C) is the ideal temperature for the hot water mixing stage.
  • 5 minutes is all you need to make a batch of simple syrup for easy mixing.
  • Whole milk is considered best for texture, though 2% or oat milk are great alternatives.
  • Sifting the powder before whisking is the best way to prevent clumps in cold drinks.
Recipe Details
Ingredient Quantity
Matcha Powder 1½ tsp
Hot Water 2 tbsp
Milk ¾ cup
Simple Syrup 1-2 tsp

Preparation Time: 5 minutes

Serving Suggestions: Enjoy as a refreshing afternoon energy booster.

Strawberry Matcha Latte: A Colorful Fruit Infusion

This vibrant beverage offers the perfect balance of ripe, aromatic berries and bright, rich green tea. With colorful layers of sweet strawberry purée and smooth, savory matcha, it is as visually attractive as it is delicious.

Where the garden meets the grove. Sip through layers of sun-ripened berry sweetness and the sophisticated depth of premium green tea.

- Danica Guariño, The Matcha Expert

Ingredients:

  • Frozen Strawberries: 1/2 cup (for purée)
  • Matcha Powder: 1½ teaspoons
  • Hot Water: 2 tablespoons (never boiling)
  • Milk: 1 cup (Whole or Plant-based)

Preparation:

  1. Blend strawberries, sugar, and water to create a smooth strawberry purée.
  2. Spoon the purée into a tall glass and gently add ice cubes on top.
  3. Sift matcha into a bowl and whisk with hot water until smooth and frothy.
  4. Slowly pour milk over the ice, then slowly pour the matcha over the surface.
  5. Add a straw, stir to combine the colorful layers, and enjoy!

Cooking Tips:

  • 5 minutes is all it takes to whip up this cafe-style beverage at home.
  • 1 instant-read thermometer helps ensure the water is the right temperature to bring out the umami.
  • Fresh strawberries can be used instead of frozen if they are ripe and in season.
  • Never use boiling water when mixing matcha to avoid ruining the delicate aroma.
Recipe Details
Ingredient Quantity
Strawberry Purée 3-4 tbsp
Matcha Powder 1½ tsp
Hot Water 2 tbsp
Milk 1 cup

Preparation Time: 10 minutes

Serving Suggestions: Best served in a tall clear glass to show off the layers.

Afternoon Indulgence: Baked Goods

Matcha Basque Cheesecake: A Burnt Green Tea Delight

Matcha Basque Burnt Cheesecake is creamy and custardy with caramel notes and an earthy sweetness. Its iconic deep-brown top contrasts beautifully with the vibrant green tea color inside. This Japanese-style cheesecake is lighter than the standard version and is surprisingly easy to make.

Defiantly burnt, yet delicately soft. A caramelized exterior hides a molten, matcha-infused heart that redefines what a cheesecake can be.

- Danica Guariño, The Matcha Expert

Ingredients:

  • Cream Cheese: 1/2 lb (at room temperature)
  • Matcha Powder: 1 tablespoon (Ceremonial or Culinary grade)
  • Eggs: 2 large plus 1 yolk
  • Heavy Cream: 220 ml (approx. 1 cup)

Preparation:

  1. Preheat oven to 450ºF (230ºC) for convection or 475ºF (245ºC) for conventional.
  2. Blend softened cream cheese with sugar until smooth and fluffy.
  3. Gradually incorporate eggs, then whisk in the matcha paste and heavy cream.
  4. Pour batter into a parchment-lined 6-inch pan and tap to remove air bubbles.
  5. Bake for 30 minutes until the top is dark brown but the center is still wobbly.

Cooking Tips:

  • 30 minutes of preheating is essential because the oven must be very hot to achieve the burnt top.
  • 10 important tips include using room temperature ingredients to ensure a perfectly smooth, lump-free batter.
  • 6-inch round cake pans are the ideal size for this recipe to ensure a rich, luscious texture.
  • 2-3 hours of cooling time is required before serving to allow the custardy center to set properly.
Recipe Details
Ingredient Quantity
Cream Cheese 1/2 lb
Matcha Powder 1 tbsp
Heavy Cream 220 ml
Eggs 2 large + 1 yolk

Preparation Time: 1 hour

Serving Suggestions: Warm your knife with hot water before slicing for a perfectly clean cut.

Matcha Marble Pound Cake: Emerald Swirl Perfection

With rich green tea swirls, this light and tender Matcha Marble Pound Cake is a delectable snack that pairs perfectly with afternoon tea. This Japanese-style pound cake is characterized by a tighter crumb and holds its shape beautifully when sliced.

Art you can eat. Every slice reveals a unique emerald swirl, offering a tender crumb that pairs perfectly with your favorite tea.

- Danica Guariño, The Matcha Expert

Ingredients:

  • Unsalted Butter: 5.6 oz (at room temperature)
  • Cake Flour: 200 g (Super-fine preferred)
  • Matcha Powder: 3 tablespoons
  • Eggs: 4 large (at room temperature)

Preparation:

  1. Cream room-temperature butter and sugar until light, fluffy, and pale.
  2. Add whisked eggs 1 tablespoon at a time to prevent the mixture from curdling.
  3. Fold in sifted dry ingredients in three stages until the batter is just smooth.
  4. Mix matcha with warm milk and combine with 1/3 of the batter; swirl into the main batter.
  5. Bake at 340ºF (170ºC) for 50-60 minutes, scoring the top after 15 minutes.

Cooking Tips:

  • 2 hours is the total time needed, but it's best eaten the next day to allow flavors to meld.
  • 15 minutes into baking, scoring the top with a sharp knife ensures a perfectly centered rustic crack.
  • Room temperature ingredients are critical; cold eggs can be warmed in body-temperature water for 10 minutes.
  • Cake flour is always used in Japanese pound cakes to achieve an extra tender, fine-crumb texture.
Recipe Details
Ingredient Quantity
Unsalted Butter 5.6 oz
Cake Flour 200 g
Matcha Powder 3 tbsp
Eggs 4 large

Preparation Time: 2 hours

Serving Suggestions: Tightly wrap and store for 24 hours before serving for a moister texture.

Matcha Gateau au Chocolat: A Velvety Green Tea Melt

A Japanese rendition of the classic French chocolate cake, this dessert is rich, moist, and intensely flavorful. Made with white chocolate and premium green tea powder, it offers a smooth, heavenly texture that melts in your mouth.

A love letter to white chocolate and green tea. This fudgy, dense delight melts instantly, leaving behind a lingering notes of umami and cocoa.

- Danica Guariño, The Matcha Expert

Ingredients:

  • White Chocolate: 3.5 oz (Baking quality)
  • Unsalted Butter: 3 tablespoons
  • Matcha Powder: 3 tablespoons
  • Eggs: 3 large (Separated)

Preparation:

  1. Melt white chocolate and butter together using a double boiler until completely smooth.
  2. Whisk egg yolks with sugar until pale, then incorporate the matcha-chocolate mixture.
  3. Prepare a glossy meringue with egg whites and gently fold into the chocolate base.
  4. Bake at 350ºF (180ºC) for 15 minutes, then reduce to 325ºF (160ºC) for 20 minutes.
  5. Cool completely for 4 hours to allow the wobbly, rich center to set properly.

Cooking Tips:

  • 55 minutes of active time, but 4 hours of cooling is mandatory for the perfect texture.
  • 2 baking temperatures are required: start high for structure, then lower to finish the center.
  • 5 seconds in the microwave for a chilled slice creates a "lava-like" experience that melts in your mouth.
  • Chilling egg whites in the freezer for 15 minutes before whipping helps create a finer, more stable meringue.
Recipe Details
Ingredient Quantity
White Chocolate 3.5 oz
Unsalted Butter 3 tbsp
Matcha Powder 3 tbsp
Eggs 3 large

Preparation Time: 55 minutes

Serving Suggestions: Dust with confectioners' sugar and serve slightly warm.

Evening Elegance: Chilled Desserts

Matcha Chocolate: A Decadent Velvet Truffle

Matcha Chocolate (Nama Chocolate) are rich squares of white chocolate and cream infused with premium Japanese green tea powder. With a truffle-like texture that melts instantly on the tongue, these decadent squares are a popular gift and a staple of Japanese confectionery.

The ultimate velvet truffle. These silk-smooth squares are a masterclass in Japanese confectionery—rich, dark, and dangerously decadent.

- Danica Guariño, The Matcha Expert

Ingredients:

  • White Chocolate: 14 oz (High-quality with cocoa butter)
  • Heavy Whipping Cream: 1/2 cup (38% fat content)
  • Unsalted Butter: 2 tablespoons
  • Matcha Powder: 2 tablespoons + 2 teaspoons for dusting

Preparation:

  1. Finely chop the white chocolate and butter to ensure they melt quickly and evenly.
  2. Heat heavy cream in a saucepan until bubbles appear around the edges; do not let it boil.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in chocolate and butter until the mixture is glossy and smooth.
  4. Sift in the matcha powder and whisk until the color is a vibrant, homogeneous green.
  5. Pour into a parchment-lined 8-inch square dish, chill for 5 hours, then slice into squares.

Cooking Tips:

  • 5 hours of chilling is required to reach the signature "Nama" (raw/fresh) truffle consistency.
  • 1 warm knife (dipped in hot water and wiped dry) is the secret to getting perfectly clean, sharp edges when slicing.
  • 38% fat content in your heavy cream is essential for the chocolate to set with the correct richness.
  • Dry utensils are mandatory; even a single drop of water can cause the white chocolate to seize and become grainy.
Recipe Details
Ingredient Quantity
White Chocolate 14 oz
Heavy Cream 1/2 cup
Matcha Powder 2 tbsp + 2 tsp
Unsalted Butter 2 tbsp

Preparation Time: 30 minutes (plus chilling)

Serving Suggestions: Dust with matcha just before serving and keep chilled until the very last moment.

Matcha Ice Cream: An Authentic Japanese Classic

This recipe captures the deep intensity and creamy texture of authentic Japanese green tea ice cream. Unlike commercial versions that are overly sweet, this homemade treat balances the earthy, robust profile of matcha with a smooth, eggless dairy base.

Purity in every scoop. No artificial colors, no shortcuts—just the bold, cooling intensity of authentic Japanese matcha.

- Danica Guariño, The Matcha Expert

Ingredients:

  • Matcha Powder: 5 tablespoons (Ceremonial or Culinary grade)
  • Whole Milk: 2¼ cups
  • Heavy Whipping Cream: ¾ cup
  • Sugar & Salt: ¾ cup sugar and ⅛ teaspoon kosher salt

Preparation:

  1. Heat milk, cream, sugar, and salt in a saucepan over medium-low until sugar dissolves; do not boil.
  2. Create a smooth paste by slowly whisking small amounts of the warm milk into the matcha powder.
  3. Combine the matcha paste with the rest of the milk mixture and strain through a fine-mesh sieve.
  4. Chill the mixture in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours until thoroughly cold.
  5. Churn in an ice cream maker for 20-25 minutes, then freeze for 4 hours until firm.

Cooking Tips:

  • 8 hours is the total time needed for chilling, churning, and freezing to develop the best flavor.
  • 4 milk options exist: you can swap the dairy for 3 cups of Half-and-Half if preferred.
  • 24 hours of pre-freezing your ice cream bowl is critical to prevent the mixture from staying liquid.
  • 5 tablespoons of matcha ensures a bold, authentic Japanese profile that isn't masked by the cream.
Recipe Details
Ingredient Quantity
Matcha Powder 5 tbsp
Milk Mixture 3 cups
Sugar ¾ cup
Kosher Salt ⅛ tsp

Preparation Time: 35 minutes (plus freezing)

Serving Suggestions: Serve in a chilled glass bowl to slow down melting on hot days.

Matcha Tiramisu: An Elegant East-West Fusion

This irresistible dessert layers green tea-soaked ladyfingers with a light, airy mascarpone cream. It is a modern Japanese twist on the classic Italian favorite, offering a sophisticated balance of creamy sweetness and the pleasant bitterness of ceremonial matcha.

An elegant fusion of two worlds. Trade the espresso for emerald tea and discover a lighter, more floral side of the Italian masterpiece.

- Danica Guariño, The Matcha Expert

Ingredients:

  • Mascarpone Cheese: 8 oz (at room temperature)
  • Egg Yolks: 3 large (cooked to 160ºF)
  • Heavy Cream: 1½ cups (chilled)
  • Ladyfingers: 24 cookies (Biscotti Savoiardi)

Preparation:

  1. Whisk egg yolks, sugar, and Marsala wine over a double boiler until they reach 160ºF (71ºC) to ensure they are safe to consume.
  2. Immediately cool the egg mixture in an ice bath, then gently fold into softened mascarpone cheese.
  3. Whip heavy cream to medium peaks and fold into the mascarpone base until smooth and airy.
  4. Quickly dip ladyfingers into a matcha solution and layer them in a dish, topping with the cream.
  5. Repeat layers and chill for at least 4 hours (preferably overnight) before dusting with matcha powder.

Cooking Tips:

  • 160 degrees Fahrenheit is the magic number for the egg yolks; use an instant-read thermometer to avoid making scrambled eggs.
  • 1 electric hand mixer is highly recommended to save your arms during the 10-minute yolk-whisking process.
  • 3 layers instead of two can be achieved by using 36 ladyfingers if you prefer more texture and less cream.
  • Right before serving is the only time you should dust the final layer of matcha to prevent it from becoming damp and dark.
Recipe Details
Ingredient Quantity
Mascarpone 8 oz
Heavy Cream 1½ cups
Egg Yolks 3 large
Ladyfingers 24-36 pcs

Preparation Time: 1 hour (plus chilling)

Serving Suggestions: Serve in individual glass cups to show off the beautiful green and white layers.
